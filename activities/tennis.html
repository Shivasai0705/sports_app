Importance of the Game

Tennis is a globally celebrated individual and doubles sport known for its grace, speed, and mental sharpness. It's not only a high-performance sport at the professional level but also a recreational activity suitable for all ages.

Playing tennis enhances concentration, builds discipline, and promotes strategic thinking. It’s a lifetime sport, providing physical benefits while reducing stress and improving social interaction through doubles play or clubs.

Materials Required

  • Tennis Racket: Typically 27 inches long, with a lightweight frame and stringed face to strike the ball effectively.
  • Tennis Balls: Yellow pressurized balls made of rubber and felt, standardized for bounce and play.
  • Tennis Court: Made of grass, clay, hard, or synthetic surfaces with nets in the center.
  • Sports Shoes: Non-marking, cushioned shoes designed to handle lateral movement on courts.
  • Apparel: Lightweight, moisture-wicking t-shirts, skirts/shorts, and optional headbands or wristbands.

Physical Benefits

Tennis provides a mix of aerobic and anaerobic workouts that contribute to a well-rounded fitness level:

  • Improves Cardiovascular Health: Sustained rallies and match duration improve heart health and stamina.
  • Builds Muscular Endurance: Strengthens legs, shoulders, arms, and core with running, serving, and swinging.
  • Increases Agility & Reflexes: Rapid direction changes and split-second reaction time sharpen coordination.
  • Enhances Flexibility: Stretching for wide shots and overhead serves increases mobility.
  • Mental Strength: Demands focus, patience, and tactical decision-making under pressure.
  • Social Well-being: Playing doubles or joining clubs enhances teamwork and interpersonal skills.

Calories Burned

Playing tennis burns 400–600 calories/hour for singles, and 300–500 calories/hour for doubles.

Recommended Age

Suitable for ages 5 and above. Kids can start with mini nets and soft balls.

Required Equipment

Tennis racket, balls, court, shoes, and apparel. Optional: sweatbands, visors, vibration dampeners.

Tennis Court Layout

Dimensions: A standard tennis court is 78 feet (23.77 meters) long and 27 feet (8.23 meters) wide for singles, and 36 feet (10.97 meters) wide for doubles.

Key Areas:

  • Baseline
  • Service Boxes
  • Singles and Doubles Sidelines
  • Center Service Line
  • Net (3 feet high in center)
  • No-Man’s Land (mid-court area)

The markings define serving zones, play boundaries, and help maintain fair competition during rallies and serves.

Rules of the Game

Governed by the International Tennis Federation (ITF), tennis follows a unique scoring and match format:

  • Scoring: Points go 15 → 30 → 40 → Game. If tied at 40, it’s called "Deuce". Players must win by two consecutive points.
  • Sets & Matches: A match is best of 3 or 5 sets. A player must win 6 games to win a set, with at least 2-game lead.
  • Serve: The server must hit diagonally into the opponent’s service box. Two chances are allowed per serve.
  • Let: A serve that hits the net and lands in the correct service box is replayed.
  • Fault: If the serve misses the service box twice, it's a double fault and point goes to opponent.
  • Ball In/Out: A ball that lands outside the boundary lines is "Out". Line calls are crucial in professional matches.
  • Volleying: Allowed, but not before the ball crosses the net unless done after a bounce on own side.
  • Tiebreaker: Used when score reaches 6–6 in a set (7-point format in most tournaments).

Tennis emphasizes fair play, etiquette, and sportsmanship. Quiet during points, shaking hands after matches, and respect for line calls are part of tennis culture.

For full rules, visit ITF Official Website or ATP Tour.
