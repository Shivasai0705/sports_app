Importance of the Game

Kabaddi is a traditional South Asian contact sport that combines elements of wrestling and tag. It's known as the "game of the masses" and requires both physical prowess and mental strategy. Kabaddi promotes agility, strength, breath control, and teamwork while being deeply rooted in cultural heritage.

The game develops lung capacity through its unique "cant" (holding breath while raiding) requirement. It enhances reflexes, strategic thinking, and physical endurance. Kabaddi has gained international recognition through events like the Pro Kabaddi League and Kabaddi World Cup.

Materials Required

  • Playing Area: Court measuring 13m × 10m for men, 12m × 8m for women
  • Comfortable Clothing: Lightweight t-shirt and shorts (often cotton)
  • Sports Shoes: Flexible shoes with good grip
  • Knee/Elbow Pads: Optional protective gear
  • Chalk/Paint: For marking court boundaries
  • Scoreboard: To track points and time

Physical Benefits

Kabaddi is an intense full-body workout with numerous health benefits:

  • Improves Lung Capacity: The cant (holding breath) technique strengthens respiratory muscles.
  • Enhances Reflexes: Quick dodging and tagging develop fast reactions.
  • Builds Strength & Endurance: Engages all muscle groups through grappling and running.
  • Boosts Flexibility: Stretching movements improve overall flexibility.
  • Develops Mental Toughness: Requires focus, strategy, and quick decision-making under pressure.

Calories Burned

Kabaddi can burn 500-800 calories per hour due to its high-intensity nature.

Recommended Age

Can be played from age 10+. Professional players typically compete between 18-35 years.

Required Equipment

Minimal equipment needed - just a marked court, comfortable clothing, and proper footwear.

Kabaddi Court Layout

Dimensions: Standard men's court is 13m × 10m, women's is 12m × 8m.

Important Zones:

  • Playing Area (main court)
  • Lobby Area (1m wide on both sides)
  • Mid Line (divides teams)
  • Baulk Line (3.75m from midline)
  • Bonus Line (1m from baulk line)
  • Sitting Block (for out players)

All lines are 5cm wide and clearly marked. The surface should be soft (often mud or synthetic mat).

Rules of the Game

Governed by the International Kabaddi Federation, the rules ensure fair and exciting gameplay:

  • Matches consist of two halves of 20 minutes each with a 5-minute halftime.
  • Each team has 7 players on court and 3-5 substitutes.
  • The raider must cross the baulk line to score points while chanting "kabaddi" without taking a breath.
  • Defenders try to stop the raider by tackling or restraining them.
  • Points are scored when:
    • Raider touches opponents and returns to their half (1 point per player touched)
    • Defenders stop the raider (1 point to defending team)
    • All opponents are out (2 bonus points)
  • Out Players must leave the court but can return when their team scores points.
  • Super Tackle: When 3 or fewer defenders stop a raider (extra point).
  • Do-or-Die Raid: After two empty raids, the third raid must score or the raider is out.

For official details, visit International Kabaddi Federation.
