Importance of the Game

Volleyball is a dynamic team sport that combines athleticism, strategy, and teamwork. Played by over 800 million people worldwide, it improves hand-eye coordination, reflexes, and cardiovascular health. Volleyball can be played indoors, on the beach, or even recreationally in backyards.

The sport teaches valuable skills like communication, quick decision-making, and spatial awareness. With major international competitions like the FIVB World Championships and Olympic events, volleyball has become one of the most popular global sports.

Materials Required

  • Volleyball: Circumference of 65-67 cm, weight of 260-280g
  • Net System: 2.43m high for men, 2.24m for women (9.5m × 1m)
  • Court: 18m × 9m with clear boundary markings
  • Knee Pads: For protection during dives
  • Proper Footwear: Volleyball shoes with gum rubber soles
  • Antennas: Flexible rods mounted on the net

Physical Benefits

Volleyball provides a full-body workout with numerous health advantages:

  • Improves Cardiovascular Health: Constant movement boosts heart rate
  • Enhances Hand-Eye Coordination: Precise ball control develops motor skills
  • Builds Upper Body Strength: Spiking and serving strengthen arms/shoulders
  • Increases Reaction Time: Quick responses to fast-moving ball
  • Burns Calories: High-energy gameplay promotes weight management

Calories Burned

Volleyball burns 300-600 calories per hour depending on intensity.

Recommended Age

Can be played from age 8 through senior years in adapted forms.

Team Composition

6 players per side (indoor), 2 players per side (beach).

Volleyball Court Layout

Dimensions: 18m × 9m (59' × 29.5') divided into two 9m × 9m squares.

Key Zones:

  • Front Zone (3m from net)
  • Back Zone
  • Attack Line (3m line)
  • Service Zone (9m deep)
  • Free Zone (3m minimum around court)

All lines are 5cm wide. The net height varies by competition level and gender.

Rules of the Game

Governed by the FIVB (Fédération Internationale de Volleyball), the main rules include:

  • Matches are best-of-5 sets (first to 25 points, 15 in deciding set)
  • Each team gets 3 touches before returning the ball
  • Scoring: Rally scoring - point on every serve
  • Rotation: Players must rotate clockwise after winning serve
  • Net Violations: No touching net during play
  • Boundaries: Ball is in if any part touches the line
  • Libero: Defensive specialist with different substitution rules
  • Timeouts: 2 per set (30 seconds each) plus technical timeouts

For official rules, visit FIVB's official website.
